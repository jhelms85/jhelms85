About Page

The guitar is thought to have originated in early 16th century Spain. Early adaptations had four strings, and closely resembled the lute. A fifth string was added around or before 1600, and by the late 18th century, the sixth string was added. This six string variation is still widely used today.

The electric guitar was said to have been born around 1936. A jazz player by the name of Charlie Christian placed a pickup in an acoustic guitar. A pickup is, in the simplest form, a magnet with a wire coil around it. They are used to amplify the vibrations of the strings.
