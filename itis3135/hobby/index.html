The guitar, a versatile plucked string instrument, typically has six strings and can be acoustic or electric. It's constructed of wood with strings made from typically nylon, or steel, and notes are produced by pressing strings against frets. Popular in various music genres worldwide, it originated in Spain and has influenced jazz, blues, folk, and more.

Electric guitars have solid bodies, and played a key role in shaping rock, blues, and pop music. With countless variations, the guitar is adaptable to almost any musical style.

When Page

AI Prompt: "An acoustic guitar that incorporates music".

The guitar is thought to have originated in early 16th century Spain. Early adaptations had four strings, and closely resembled the lute. A fifth string was added around or before 1600, and by the late 18th century, the sixth string was added. This six string variation is still widely used today.

The electric guitar was said to have been born around 1936. A jazz player by the name of Charlie Christian placed a pickup in an acoustic guitar. A pickup is, in the simplest form, a magnet with a wire coil around it. They are used to amplify the vibrations of the strings.

Information from: Britannica and Yamaha.

Two common Tunings for six-string guitars:

E standard tuning is the standard tuning for a guitar, where the strings are tuned to E-A-D-G-B-E from low to high pitch.

Eb standard tuning, also known as "half step down," lowers the pitch of all strings by a half step, resulting in the tuning Eb-Ab-Db-Gb-Bb-Eb.
